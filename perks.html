Analysis of The Perks of Being a Wallflower



The complex and sensitive topics of Stephen Chbosky's The Perks of Being a Wallflower, such as sexual assault, drug misuse, mental health issues, and homosexuality, have led to several challenges and library and school bans. Charlie, an emotionally vulnerable and introverted youngster, is followed throughout the book as he deals with the effects of prior abuse while navigating friendships, pain, love, and self-discovery. The book's handling of these serious subjects, which include graphic language and teen experiences, has caused critics to declare it "obscene" or unsuitable for young audiences, which has resulted in its removal from classrooms and libraries.

For example, the Highland Park Independent School District in Texas banned it in 2015, and it was challenged in many other places due to allegations of "child pornography" and how it handled delicate subjects (Marshall University Libraries). Despite the difficulties, the book is regarded as a classic of young adult literature and has received praises for its realistic portrayal of puberty and mental health.
